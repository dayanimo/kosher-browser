Privacy Policy — Kosher Browser

Last updated: 2026-05-01

Kosher Browser is a Chrome extension that helps you block distracting or inappropriate websites with schedules, a focus timer, password protection, and SafeSearch enforcement.

What we collect

Nothing. Kosher Browser does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. No analytics. No telemetry. No remote servers.

What is stored locally

The extension stores the following information only on your own device, using Chrome's local extension storage:

  • Your blocklist, allowlist, category preferences, and schedule.
  • Whether the focus timer is active and when it ends.
  • A cryptographic hash of your password (if you set one). The plaintext password is never stored.
  • Counts of how many sites have been blocked (for the in-extension statistics view).
  • Your interface language preference (Hebrew or English).
  • Whether SafeSearch enforcement is on.

This data never leaves your computer.

Permissions and why we need them

  • storage — to save your settings on your device.
  • alarms — to check your schedule windows and end focus sessions on time.
  • declarativeNetRequest, declarativeNetRequestWithHostAccess — to block and redirect requests to sites you've configured.
  • host_permissions: <all_urls> — required by Chrome so the extension can redirect blocked sites on any domain to the in-extension block page. The extension does not read page contents, modify pages, or inject scripts into web pages.

Third parties

None. Kosher Browser does not integrate with any third-party service.

Children

Kosher Browser does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes

If we ever change this policy, we'll update the "Last updated" date above and publish the new version at the same URL.
